# Term Life Insurance, What It Is and How Much Cover You Need

## What term insurance is

Term insurance is pure life cover. You pay a premium; if you die during the policy term, your family receives the sum assured; if you outlive the term, you get nothing back. That "nothing back" is precisely why it is cheap: all of the premium buys protection, none of it is diverted into a low-return savings pot.

Because of this, term insurance offers the largest cover per rupee of premium of any life product. A healthy 30-year-old can typically buy Rs 1 crore of cover for a few hundred rupees a month, an amount no endowment or money-back policy can approach.

## Who needs it, and who does not

Term insurance replaces your income for people who depend on it. You need it if anyone, a spouse, children, parents, or a co-borrower on a loan, would face financial hardship without your earnings.

You do not need it if nobody depends on your income: a single person with no dependants and no large debts, or a retiree whose children are independent, gets little from life cover. Insurance is for dependants, not a rite of passage.

## How much cover: the 8-10x rule plus debts

The widely used sizing rule:

Cover = 8 to 10 times annual income + all outstanding loans

The income multiple is meant to let the family invest the payout and draw an income from it for many years. The debt add-on ensures a home loan or other liability does not consume the payout meant for living expenses.

Example: annual income Rs 12,00,000 and an outstanding home loan of Rs 30,00,000 points to cover of roughly Rs 1.2 to 1.5 crore. Round up rather than down; cover erodes in real terms with inflation every year.

## Choosing the term and the policy

- Run the policy until your planned retirement age or until dependants become independent, commonly age 60 to 65. Beyond that, income replacement is no longer the need.
- Prefer a plain term plan. Return-of-premium variants refund your premiums at maturity but charge two to three times more; the extra premium invested separately almost always does better.
- Disclose everything honestly: smoking, health conditions, existing policies. Non-disclosure is the leading cause of claim rejection, and it surfaces at the worst possible time.
- Check the insurer's claim settlement ratio and fill the nomination correctly.

## When to review

Revisit cover after every major life event: marriage, a child, a new home loan, or a large income jump. Each of these raises the income to be replaced or the debt to be cleared, and a top-up term plan is usually cheap to add while you are young.
