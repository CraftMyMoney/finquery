# Health Insurance Basics and How Much Cover You Need

## Why health cover comes before investing

A single hospitalization can cost more than a year of savings. Medical inflation in India runs well above general inflation, with hospital costs rising roughly 12 to 14 percent a year. Without insurance, a health event is paid for by breaking investments or taking on debt, which is why protection is sequenced before wealth building in most planning frameworks.

## How much cover

Cover needs scale with hospital costs in your city:

- **Metro cities** (Mumbai, Delhi, Bengaluru, and similar): around Rs 15,00,000 of family cover.
- **Tier 2 cities**: around Rs 10,00,000.
- **Smaller towns**: around Rs 5,00,000.

These are floor numbers for a family floater covering a couple and children; add more for older parents, who are best covered under a separate policy so their claims do not exhaust the family's shared cover.

A cost-efficient way to reach high cover is a base policy plus a **super top-up**: for example, a Rs 5,00,000 base policy with a Rs 20,00,000 super top-up that activates above the base, at a much lower premium than a single Rs 25,00,000 policy.

## Corporate cover is not enough

Employer group insurance is a real benefit but a fragile one: it ends the day you leave the job, precisely when income has also stopped; the sum insured is often Rs 3,00,000 to Rs 5,00,000, below metro costs; and you cannot control renewals or exclusions. The standard advice is to hold a personal policy in addition to corporate cover. Buying personal cover while young and healthy also locks in insurability before conditions like diabetes or hypertension appear, after which policies get costly or carry exclusions.

## Reading the policy: what actually matters

- **Waiting periods**: pre-existing conditions are typically covered only after 2 to 4 years; specific procedures have their own waits.
- **Room rent limits**: a cap like 1 percent of sum insured per day silently scales down the entire claim if you occupy a costlier room. Prefer no-cap policies.
- **Co-payment clauses**: a 10 or 20 percent co-pay means you share every bill; common in senior citizen policies.
- **Sub-limits and exclusions**: caps on specific treatments, and lists of what is never covered.
- **Cashless network**: check that hospitals near you are on the insurer's list.

## Keep the policy alive

Renew on time every year; a lapse can restart waiting periods. Declare pre-existing conditions honestly at purchase, since non-disclosure is the most common reason claims fail. Use the free annual health checkups many policies include, and review the sum insured every few years against rising costs.
