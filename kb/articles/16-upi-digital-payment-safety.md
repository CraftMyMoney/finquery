# UPI and Digital Payment Safety

## The one rule that stops most UPI fraud

**You never need to enter your UPI PIN to receive money.** The PIN authorizes money going out, only and always. Nearly every UPI scam is a costume around tricking you into an outgoing action while believing it is incoming: fake "collect requests" for a lottery or cashback, a "refund" that is actually a payment request, or a fraudster on a call walking you through screens until you approve a debit.

Corollaries: scanning a QR code is for **paying**, never for receiving; and an OTP or PIN is never required to accept a credit. Anyone who says otherwise, however official they sound, is describing a debit.

## The common scam patterns

- **Collect-request fraud**: a request arrives dressed as a prize, refund, or verification; approving it sends money out.
- **Fake customer care**: search results and social media reply-guys impersonating bank or app helplines, asking for PIN, OTP, or a screen-share app (AnyDesk, TeamViewer). Real support never needs any of these.
- **Fraud loop after a genuine mistake**: you send money to the wrong VPA, search for a helpline, and the fake helpline defrauds you a second time.
- **Cheap-deal QR codes**: marketplace "buyers" who send a QR code to "pay you the advance"; scanning and entering the PIN pays them.
- **SIM and device takeover**: phishing links that install apps reading OTPs.

## Hygiene that actually matters

1. Keep the UPI PIN as private as an ATM PIN; no bank, app, police officer, or "RBI official" will ever ask for it.
2. Verify the receiver's name shown by the app before every payment; a VPA is easy to mistype, and the confirmation screen is the last checkpoint.
3. Set app-level and account-level transaction limits; keep a low-balance account linked for daily UPI use rather than the main savings account.
4. Use only official apps from official stores, lock the phone and the payment app, and never install screen-share tools on a stranger's instruction.
5. Ignore urgency. Every fraud script manufactures a deadline: an expiring reward, a blocked account, an arriving penalty. Legitimate institutions do not operate at scam tempo.

## If money leaves anyway

Speed determines recovery odds. Immediately: report inside the UPI app, call your bank's official number (from the card or bank website, not a search result) to block further debits, and file at the national cyber crime helpline **1930** or cybercrime.gov.in. Banks and NPCI have a defined chargeback and complaint chain: the sooner it starts, the higher the chance funds are frozen before withdrawal. RBI's limited-liability rules also protect customers who report unauthorized transactions promptly; delay shifts liability toward the customer.
