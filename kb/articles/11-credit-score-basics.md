# Credit Scores in India, What Moves Your CIBIL Score

## What the score is

A credit score is a three-digit number, from 300 to 900 in India, summarizing how you have handled borrowed money. It is computed by credit bureaus (TransUnion CIBIL is the best known; Experian, Equifax, and CRIF High Mark also operate) from data that lenders report about your loans and credit cards.

Rough bands: **750 and above** is considered good to excellent and gets loans approved at the best rates; 650 to 750 gets scrutiny and sometimes worse pricing; below 650 makes new credit difficult. A person with no credit history has no score, which itself makes lenders cautious.

## What moves the score

- **Payment history (largest factor)**: every EMI and credit card bill paid on time helps; a single payment overdue by 30+ days hurts for a long time, and settlements or write-offs hurt most.
- **Credit utilization**: the share of your credit card limits you actually use. Keeping utilization **below 30 percent** of the limit reads as controlled; consistently maxed cards read as stress even when paid in full.
- **Credit mix and age**: a mix of secured (home, auto) and unsecured (cards, personal loans) credit with a long history helps. Closing your oldest card shortens visible history.
- **Hard inquiries**: every loan or card application triggers a lender pull. Many applications in a short window look like credit hunger and shave points. Shopping for one loan is fine; scattering applications is not.

## What does not affect it

Income, savings balances, investments, and debit card usage are not in the score; the bureau only sees credit. Checking your own score is a soft inquiry and never hurts it.

## Improving a score, and the timeline

There is no fast fix; the levers are mechanical:

1. Automate every EMI and card payment; even one missed due date resets progress.
2. Bring card utilization down, by spending less on the card or requesting a higher limit while spending the same.
3. Stop new applications for six months.
4. Check your report for errors, a loan you closed showing open, an account that is not yours, and dispute them with the bureau; each bureau must provide one free report per year.

Meaningful movement takes 3 to 6 months of clean behaviour; recovery from a default takes years, and the record of the default stays on the report for up to seven.

## Why it matters beyond approval

The score prices your debt. On a Rs 50,00,000 home loan, the interest rate difference between an excellent and a mediocre score can exceed the cost of a year of EMIs over the loan's life. Guarding the score is cheaper than buying it back.
